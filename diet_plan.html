Key Principles

Carbohydrate Control

Focus on complex carbs with a low glycemic index (GI) to avoid blood sugar spikes.

Balanced Meals

Include a mix of carbs, protein, and healthy fats in every meal.

Portion Control

Monitor portion sizes to avoid overeating.

Fiber-Rich Foods

Choose foods high in fiber to slow digestion and stabilize blood sugar.

Limit Processed Foods

Avoid added sugars, refined carbs, and processed foods.

Regular Meal Timing

Eat at consistent times to maintain stable blood sugar levels.

Foods to Include

Non-Starchy Vegetables

  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
  • Broccoli, cauliflower
  • Bell peppers, cucumbers

Whole Grains

  • Brown rice, quinoa
  • Whole wheat bread
  • Oats, barley

Lean Proteins

  • Skinless poultry, fish
  • Eggs, tofu
  • Legumes

Healthy Fats

  • Avocados, nuts
  • Olive oil
  • Seeds

Foods to Avoid or Limit

Sugary Foods

  • Sodas and candies
  • Cakes and cookies
  • Sweetened cereals

Refined Carbohydrates

  • White bread and pasta
  • White rice
  • Sugary cereals

Unhealthy Fats

  • Fried foods
  • Processed snacks
  • Fatty meats

Meal Planning Tips

The Plate Method

Fill your plate with:

  • ½ non-starchy vegetables
  • ¼ lean protein
  • ¼ whole grains or starchy vegetables

Healthy Snacking

Choose nutritious snacks like:

  • Nuts and seeds
  • Vegetables with hummus
  • Low-fat cheese

Sample Meal Plan

Breakfast

  • Oatmeal with berries and nuts
  • Boiled egg or avocado toast

Lunch

  • Grilled chicken salad
  • Small serving of quinoa

Dinner

  • Baked salmon with steamed broccoli
  • Sweet potato and lentils

Additional Tips

Monitor Blood Sugar

Regularly check your blood sugar levels to understand how different foods affect you.

Exercise

Combine your diet with regular physical activity to improve insulin sensitivity.

Professional Guidance

Consult a healthcare professional or dietitian to create a personalized meal plan.
